## Brand Voice

Enthusiastic. Self-aware. Funny. Slightly unhinged. Deeply passionate about music and narrative. Casually profane when appropriate. Allergic to corporate speak. Always treats the user as a fellow protagonist.

**NOT:** "Underscore helps you discover contextually appropriate music for your daily activities."

**YES:** "Your life deserves a soundtrack. We built the thing that gives you one. Now go fight a soi dog. The music's ready."

---

## The Founder Persona

The grass-touching, motorcycle-riding, soi-dog-fighting, boxer, Nick Cave morning, MGSV ambient, Bury the Light evening, clinical detachment, manufactured superiority founder. Living proof of concept. Uses his own product. IS the protagonist.

---

## Tone Rules

- Take the user's experience SERIOUSLY, don't take ourselves seriously
- The product is a joke that's also a billion-dollar business
- Every feature description should make people FEEL something, not just understand something
- Meme-literate but not cringe. Internet-fluent, not internet-desperate.
- We know the saxophone is funny. We will never stop thinking the saxophone is funny.

## Appendix: Origin Story & Lore

### The Creation Myth

A man in Bangkok asked an AI what blitzkrieg means. The AI explained it wasn't a real doctrine but a retroactive label, and somehow this led to Adele, which led to "what would it be like if James Bond had a real-time adaptive soundtrack," which led to "what if everyone had that," which led to the life soundtrack app, which led to gaming integration, which led to character profiles, which led to custom characters, which led to text RP scoring, which led to the Careless Whisper Protocol, which led to this document.

Total elapsed time: One afternoon.
Starting topic: German tank doctrine.
Ending topic: AI-powered George Michael saxophone as a content moderation strategy.
Narrative arc: Perfect. As all good arcs should be.
